ANIME

Anime is animation that originates from Japan. Outside of Japan, the word "anime" typically refers to Japanese animation specifically, while in Japan, the term (a shortening of the English word "animation") refers to all animated works, regardless of origin. Anime is a diverse medium encompassing a wide range of genres, styles, and target audiences, from children to adults.

Key Aspects of Anime

Distinctive Art Style: While no single style predominates, anime often features shared visual attributes such as characters with large, expressive eyes, varied hair colors and styles, and a focus on detailed backgrounds and cinematic camera effects.

Diverse Genres and Demographics: Anime covers virtually any topic and is often classified by its target audience, including Kodomo (young children), Shōnen (pre-teen and teenage boys), Shōjo (pre-teen and teenage girls), Seinen (young men), and Josei (young women).

Storytelling Depth: Compared to much Western animation, anime frequently features complex narratives, deep character development, and a wide emotional and thematic range.

Global Influence: The medium has a massive global fanbase and has become a multi-billion dollar industry, influencing fashion, art, and popular culture worldwide.
